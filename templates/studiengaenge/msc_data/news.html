Admission

You need a Bachelor's degree with a minimum of 30 credits in Mathematics, including 12 credits in foundations of calculus and linear algebra. In english, you must have B2. You can only start this program in the winter term (October 1st).

Curriculum

You will obtain solid foundation in mathematics in a basic course, and an additional advanced course in applied mathematics. In the electives, you can choose from modules such as numerical optimization, numerics for partial differential equations, neural nets, financial mathematics, dynamical systems in biology, econometrics, computer vision, automated machine learning, etc. More information can be found here

Mathematics

We strongly believe that Mathemtics is the foundation of data science and technology. In order to provide the necessary skills areas such as numerical mathematics, optimization, and probability theory, you take part in various courses from the study program in the MSc Mathematics.

Coding

Transforming the fundamentals to applications mostly required the knowledge to showcase your project using an implementation. Although programming skills are not required upon admission, coding will be an important part of the basic module, and will be strengthened in a specific coding module.

Internship

The programming project can be exchanged by an industrial placement (interneship) of at least six weeks. As examples, we have connections to various local companies, including Sick, Baloise, LBBW, Allianz, Fraunhofer, Bosch.

Freiburg

Freiburg is a lovely town at the foot of the black forest. It is a green city with many bicycles and opportunities to spend your free time. The University of Freiburg not only has a long tradition covering all areas from science and the humanities, but is also very active in research, as shown by its success in the excellence initiative. The mathematical institute, which hosts most of this study program, covers a wide range of topics, from mathematical logic to machine learning.
